🧭 Setup Guide

  • iPhone

📶 Step 0: Insert the SIM Card

Before powering on the iPhone, please insert the SIM card as follows:

  1. Find the SIM tray on the side of the iPhone.
  2. Use the SIM ejector tool to gently push into the small hole.
  3. The tray will pop out — place the SIM card in the tray, aligning the notched corner correctly.
  4. Slide the tray back in gently until it clicks into place.

Important: Make sure the SIM card is seated properly before powering on the device.

⚙️ Step 1: Turn on and connect to Wi-Fi

1. Power on your iPhone

2. Set the language

3. Set the Region and appearance

4. Click Set up without another device

5. Select a secure Wi-Fi network (home or a personal network).

🔑 Step 2: Enrol Remote Management

Enrol the iPhone with Remote Management.

Sign in using your council email and password.

🔑 Step 3: Create an iPhone Passcode and enable Location Services

Set up Face ID (Optional)

Create a Passcode

Enable Location Services.

📲 Step 4: Company Portal App

Locate the Company Portal App and sign in using your work email and password.

📲 Step 5: Set Device Category

Device category, select

SCC Mobile Phones

Tap Continue

The phone will take a couple minutes to finsh the enrolment and check device settings.

📲 Step 6: MS Defender App

Locate the MS Defender App, select your work account to sign in

Agree to the Terms of Use and click Accept

Tap Allow Microsoft Defender to set up a local VPN connection

Tap Allow notifications.

📲 Step 7: All set

The device is ready!

It will receive regular system and security updates from Apple.

Ensure they are installed to keep the device secure and compliant.

📋 Device Usage Tips

  • Do not install unauthorised apps.

  • Keep your device locked when not in use.

How do I request an app not listed?

Log a Rubix request including the app name, store link, cost and justification. Our security team will review your request.

Outlook, Teams, Company Portal, GoBright, Authenicator, MS Defender and WhatsApp are included. Additional apps depend on your role.

Can I make personal calls or use mobile data?

No. All usage is tracked via weekly/monthly reports and must comply with council policy.

Can I sign in with my personal Apple ID or iTunes account?

No. Linking personal accounts is not permitted on council-issued devices.

How do I transfer contacts from my old Android or iPhone to my new iPhone?

Important: SCC iPhones do not support personal Apple IDs or iCloud. Please use the steps below to transfer contacts without using iCloud:

From Android to iPhone (Without iCloud)

  1. On your Android, open the Contacts app.
  2. Tap the menu (⋮ or ☰) and choose Export.
  3. Export your contacts as a .vcf file and save it locally.
  4. Email the .vcf file to your SCC email address.
  5. Important: On your new iPhone, do not tap the .vcf file directly in Outlook — this will only import the contacts into Outlook.
  6. Instead, download the .vcf file to Files, then open it from the Files app and select Add Contacts when prompted.

From another iPhone (Without iCloud)

  1. On the old iPhone, open the Contacts app.
  2. Tap your name or select All Contacts.
  3. In the top right, tap Share and select Share Contact.
  4. Choose All Contacts, then share via Outlook.
  5. This will send a .vcf file to your SCC email.
  6. Important: On your new iPhone, do not tap the .vcf in Outlook — download it via Files and open it from there to import contacts properly.
How do I check if my device is enroled correctly?

Open the Company portal app, tap the Devices Icon at the bottom, you should see the device name listed and a green checkmark or 'compliant' status.

I cant see my work apps - what should I do?

It my take a few minutes after set up to install all apps. Make sure you're connected to Wi-Fi or Mobile Data and keep the phone unlocked. If the issue persists, raise a Rubix Ticket

What should I do if the Company Portal app gets stuck or wont sign in?

Restart your phone, ensure you are connected to Wi-Fi, and try again. If the issue persists, raise a Rubix ticket.

I forgot my passcode - what should I do?

Unfortunately, IT cannot recover or bypass your passcode. The only solution is to perform a factory reset, which will erase all data and settings on the device. After resetting, you'll need to set up the phone again from scratch. Any unsaved data will be permanently lost.

How do I factory reset my phone?

Important: All files and data will be permanently erased and cannot be recovered

Go to Settings> Transfer or Reset iPhone> Click Erase All Content and Settings Click Confirm

How do I find the serial number of the phone?

Go to Settings> General> About> The serial number will be displayed on this screen.

What should I do if my phone is lost or stolen?

Log a ticket via Rubix immediatley. If stolen, include a police report reference. We will attempt a remote wipe, terminate any SIM contracts and issue a replacement if needed.

What happens if I leave the council?

Your device must be returned to IT. All council data will be wiped remotely, and the device reset before reissue.
